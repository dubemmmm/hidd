Flood risk in Lagos is often treated as a seasonal inconvenience. For buyers, that framing is too weak. Flood pressure changes **liveability, maintenance cost, tenant confidence, and long-term pricing defensibility**.

## Not all Lekki exposure is equal

Lekki Phase 1, Lekki Phase 2, Ajah, and Sangotedo should not be discussed as if they carry the same drainage performance or infrastructure maturity. Buyers need area-specific judgement rather than corridor-level generalisation.

## Ask what supports the site during failure conditions

In heavy rain events, the real question is what the site, access road, drainage system, and surrounding estate can absorb. Marketing materials rarely answer that. On-ground diligence does.

## Use flood pressure as a negotiation variable

If an asset sits in an area with recurrent flood concerns, the asking price should reflect that risk. Flood exposure is not only a safety issue. It is a value issue.
