Buying property in Lagos without a process is how buyers inherit hidden defects, title disputes, and pricing errors that were visible before the transfer. HIDD approaches each transaction with one question: **what must be true before capital should move?**

## Start with the asset, not the sales pitch

The first pass should establish whether the physical property is worth pursuing at all. If the building has structural distress, weak drainage, poor workmanship, or deferred maintenance, legal certainty alone does not make it a good acquisition.

## Validate the paperwork against the real-world site

Documents should not be reviewed in isolation. Survey plans, title paperwork, site particulars, and the actual asset need to agree with one another. When they do not, that is not administrative noise. It is transaction risk.

## Price risk properly

A seller's asking price may reflect branding, urgency, or pure optimism. Buyers need an independent sense of value and a clear picture of neighbourhood downside before they negotiate from strength.

## Build one decision pack

The strongest buyers consolidate inspection, legal diligence, area intelligence, and valuation into one commercial decision. That is how teams remove guesswork and move faster when the asset is actually sound.
