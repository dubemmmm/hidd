Strong title work is not about collecting impressive-looking paper. It is about understanding whether the seller can validly transfer the asset and whether there are hidden issues behind the document stack.

## A document is not the same thing as clean title

Buyers often confuse the existence of a deed, consent, or survey with complete legal comfort. In practice, the supporting chain, consistency, and site facts matter just as much.

## Documentation should answer commercial questions

Can the asset be transferred cleanly? Are there ownership conflicts? Is the documentation internally consistent? Are there obvious flags that would delay closing or weaken future resale? That is the standard buyers should use.

## Legal diligence is a decision tool

The goal is not paperwork for its own sake. The goal is a better acquisition decision with fewer surprises after commitment.
