Ikoyi and Victoria Island are often bundled together in buyer conversations because both sit at the premium end of the Lagos residential market. That shortcut hides meaningful differences in **use case, asset profile, and downside exposure**.

## Ikoyi typically wins on defensibility

For buyers prioritising quieter residential positioning, stronger title comfort, and more stable premium residential demand, Ikoyi often offers the more defensible long-term story.

## Victoria Island can win on commercial adjacency

Buyers who value immediate proximity to business activity, hospitality, and mixed-use demand may find Victoria Island more strategically aligned, provided the asset itself is strong.

## The asset still matters more than the postcode

No neighbourhood premium replaces inspection, legal diligence, valuation discipline, or area-level risk review. The correct decision is rarely made by location reputation alone.
